Step 2 of 7

On-Device Processing — Privacy by Destruction

All six sensor streams are processed on the Exynos W1000 chip. Raw data is born, classified, and destroyed within milliseconds. Nothing leaves the device except wellness indicator labels, event counts, and timestamps.

🔄 The On-Device Processing Pipeline — What Happens Inside the Watch
1
Raw Sensor Capture
MIC Audio waveform enters 10-second rolling buffer (continuous overwrite) · PPG Light pulse reflections from BioActive sensor · EDA Skin conductance stream at 1 Hz · ACCEL XYZ acceleration at 25 Hz · GYRO Rotation data · IR Skin surface temperature
↓
2
Own-Voice Isolation — "Whose Voice Is It?"
Layer 1 (Watch): MIC Bandpass filter isolates speech frequencies (85 Hz–4 kHz). ACCEL Accelerometer detects speech-correlated body vibrations transmitted through the wrist — when audio amplitude exceeds threshold but the accelerometer shows no speech vibration pattern, the sound is classified as environmental and ignored. Ambient noise floor is continuously updated using spectral subtraction during detected silence periods.
Layer 2 (BrooksClip — optional companion): Bone conduction vibration sensor worn near the vocal source sends a binary flag ("vocalizing" / "not vocalizing") to the watch via BLE. When the BrooksClip confirms vocalization, audio amplitude is definitively attributed to the wearer. When it signals silence, all detected audio is classified as someone else's voice or environmental sound. The BrooksClip transmits no audio — only a vibration flag.
Personal Voice Learning: During enrollment (first 3 days of wear), the device builds a personal voice profile — learning the student's unique vocal frequency range, typical speaking amplitude, and speech patterns. All thresholds adapt relative to the student's own baseline, not fixed values. The device gets more accurate at distinguishing the student's voice from classmates over time.
↓
3
Feature Extraction + SLM Calibration (On-Device Only)
MIC RMS calculation on voice-attributed audio only → dBFS conversion → SLM calibration offset applied. The watch microphone is calibrated against a reference Sound Level Meter (SLM) using an offset value (adjustable in 0.5 dB increments). The on-device app converts raw dBFS (decibels relative to digital full scale — native to the microphone) to approximate dBA (A-weighted decibels — the standard acoustic measurement scale) by applying: dBA ≈ dBFS + microphone sensitivity constant + calibration offset. Example: a raw reading of -16 dBFS with a mic sensitivity of +94 dB and a calibration offset of +2.5 dBA = approximately 80.5 dBA. This is the same offset calibration methodology used in OSHA-compliant workplace noise dosimetry. Audio content is never analyzed — only amplitude.
PPG Inter-beat interval (IBI) calculation → HRV derivation · EDA Conductance level comparison to personal baseline · ACCEL Movement pattern classification · IR Temperature delta from baseline
↓
4
Raw Data Destruction
Audio waveform: destroyed after RMS extraction — 10-second rolling buffer continuously overwrites. No audio content ever persists on device or transfers anywhere. PPG light pulses: discarded after IBI calculation. EDA raw conductance: discarded after baseline comparison. Accelerometer raw XYZ: discarded after pattern classification. Voice profile stays on-device only. No raw biometric data is ever stored or transmitted.
↓
5
Personal Baseline Comparison
Each sensor reading is compared to the student's own personal baseline (learned and continuously refined from first 3 days of wear onward). The output for each sensor is a wellness indicator label: above baseline at baseline below baseline — never a raw numeric value. Thresholds are relative to the student's own ambient floor, not absolute values — a threshold in a quiet library differs from the same threshold on a noisy playground. Environment classified as: Quiet Instruction, Group Activity, Transition, or High-Ambient.
↓
6
What Leaves the Device
Event counts (integers) · Event durations (seconds) · Timestamps · Environment labels · Biometric wellness labels per sensor (above / at / below personal baseline) — each sensor reported independently, never combined into composite scores. Synced via Knox-secured BLE at end of school day on school WiFi.
↓
7

The platform receives only labels and counts. It routes the relevant data to each professional's view: SLP sees voice + vocalization data. OT sees all six biometric streams independently. Teacher sees voice + engagement + transition data. Family sees a plain-language summary. The professional writes all documentation and applies all judgment.

👨‍👩‍👧 Family: Plain-language daily summary only. No event counts, no biometric labels, no technical terminology.
One device deployment → three professional roles served → each sees only their relevant wellness observation labels. The same Samsung Galaxy Watch. The same Knox lockdown. Three additional professional roles beyond what a single-sensor device provides.

✅ The platform observes:

• Independent timestamps for label changes
• Duration from label change to label at baseline
• Daily observation logs with teacher annotations
🚫 The platform never:
• Displays raw physiological values (BPM, ms, μS, °F)
• Calculates intervals between physiological and behavioral events
• Compares intervention days vs. non-intervention days
• Diagnoses any condition or state
• Combines biometrics into composite scores
• Recommends actions or interventions
• Uses clinical or medical language
The device captures raw signals and immediately destroys them. The platform receives labels, counts, and acoustic measurements. The AI surfaces patterns in that data. The professional provides conclusions. This boundary is maintained in every observation the platform generates.

🔒 Data Collection Controls
Each sensor stream can be independently enabled or disabled by the authorized adult (parent, guardian, or school administrator) at any time. Disabled sensors do not collect, process, or transmit any data. Changes take effect immediately on the device.
📋 Privacy Policy Summary
What is collected: Voice volume amplitude (dB), ambient sound level (dB), event timestamps, event durations, environment classification labels, and physiological wellness labels (above / at / below personal baseline for heart rate, HRV, skin conductance, skin temperature, and movement). No audio content, speech content, words, or conversation is ever recorded, stored, or transmitted.

What is NOT collected: No audio recordings. No speech content. No words or language. No raw physiological values (no BPM, ms, μS, or °F). No location data in the school deployment. No photographs or images. No student names in the data stream — all data is linked to a de-identified student code assigned by the school administrator.

On-device processing: All raw sensor signals are processed on the Samsung Galaxy Watch 8 Exynos W1000 chip and immediately destroyed. The 10-second rolling audio buffer continuously overwrites. No raw sensor data is ever stored on the device or transmitted anywhere. Only derived wellness labels, event counts, durations, and acoustic measurements (dB) leave the device.

Data transmission: Observation data syncs from the device to the school's platform instance via Knox-secured BLE connection at the end of the school day on the school's WiFi network only. Data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256). No data is transmitted to any cloud service, third party, or external server outside the school's platform instance.

Data retention: Observation data is retained for the current IEP cycle (typically one academic year) and automatically purged at the end of the retention period unless the authorized adult requests extension. Data can be deleted at any time by the authorized adult (parent/guardian) or school administrator.

Cookies and tracking: This demo uses no cookies, no tracking pixels, no analytics scripts, and no third-party code. The production platform will use only essential session cookies required for user authentication — never advertising cookies, tracking cookies, or third-party analytics. No data from the platform is shared with advertisers, data brokers, or any third party.

Opt-out: Any sensor stream can be independently disabled at any time by the authorized adult. The device can be completely deactivated by the authorized adult or school administrator at any time. All data collection ceases immediately upon deactivation. Previously collected data can be deleted upon request.
